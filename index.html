Guide
  1. Set your name and baseline. Type your name up top; set a CMJ baseline in Tests if you have one.
  2. Log every session. Type, minutes and RPE (asked ~30 min after). Load = minutes × RPE.
  3. Check in daily. Five quick 1–5 scores. They sharpen every other signal.
  4. Test now and then. Re-test the same items to build trends.
  5. Save your file weekly. It's your backup and your way to a new phone.

References & Methodology

  • Foster's sRPE: Load is calculated using Foster's session-RPE (Duration × 1-10 RPE) as a validated measure of internal training load.
  • Energy Systems: Framework mapped to ASCA Level 1 (Module 5) and NSCA guidelines (Phosphagen, Glycolytic, Aerobic).
  • Anaerobic Speed Reserve (ASR): Based on Sandford, Laursen & Buchheit (Sports Med 2021) for supra-MAS interval prescription.
  • Karvonen Formula: HR Zones utilize resting HR to track %VO2 more closely than max HR alone.
  • Maximal Aerobic Speed (MAS): Utilizes the 5-minute time trial protocol (Berthon et al., 1997).

Reading the z-scores

A z-score counts how many standard deviations a value sits from your own average. 0 is normal; 1 SD is how much your numbers usually bounce week to week. It measures how out-of-the-ordinary today is — for you, not anyone else.

0 to ±1~2 in 3 weeks. Normal — nothing to chase.
±1 to ±1.5Mildly unusual. Worth a glance.
±1.5 to ±2~1 in 15 weeks. Notably unusual — check how you feel.
beyond ±2~1 in 20 weeks. Something changed — know what before acting.

Sign is direction, not quality. Load z +2 = a much heavier week; RPE z +2 = the same session felt much harder than it usually does for you — often the more telling signal.
